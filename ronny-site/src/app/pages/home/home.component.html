Welcome!


Ronny Bazan-Antequera Assistant Teaching Professor | Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Information Technology

Hi there! My name is Ronny Bazan-Antequera, and I am an assistant teaching professor at the University of Missouri-Columbia. Prior to joining the EECS department, I worked in the industry as a Senior Systems Administrator. I received a Fullbright Scholarship from Mizzou, where I earned my Master's degree in Computer Science along with an Outstanding Master Student award. I went on to receive my PhD in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science from the University of Missouri.


At Mizzou, I teach a wide variety of Computer Science and Information Technology courses that focus on Linux Systems and Cybersecurity. These courses have been taught both online and in person, and emphasize a "learning by doing" hands-on approach. Much of this information consists of skills and abilities that employers want and expect in the real world of CS and IT in all manner of organizations. Each course has a set of 12 laboratory assignments, where students create and experiment upon virtualized computer systems, both on their own machines (VMWare Workstation/Fusion) and on the cloud (AWS). Each course also features a final project that allows students to expand on the course content and investigate a topic valuable to their own future work as CS/IT professionals.
